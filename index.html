There are so many books about composting out there… Here is a quick guide of many books about composting:

https://www.amazon.com/Compost-Family-Making-Discover-Together/dp/1611801273

This book is cute and suitable for people who want to introduce composting to kids. The book includes stickers and well-designed activities to help kids understand composting. My favorite page is page 29 where there is a spinning wheel to see which organic waste can be composted and which cannot. There is also a cute Worms and Ladders board game included in the book. Overall, I recommend this book for families with kids age 10 and younger.

https://www.amazon.com/Composting-Basics-Skills-Tools-Started/dp/0811718379/

This book provides the most graphic and detailed steps on how to build a three-bin compost system. The pictures are clear, down-to-earth, no-filter and helpful. If you are a visual learner, this book will be helpful to walk you through all the basics, how to build bins, what are the various tools, how to collect worm castings, etc.

https://www.amazon.com/Let-Rot-Composting-Down-Earth/dp/1580170234/

This book is wordy, in a good way if you genuinely want to understand the nitty gritty behind how each piece of the compost puzzle works. I imagine some people find all the texts in this book off putting. I enjoyed reading this book because it presented different composting method in details, it explains how decomposition works. If I like reading text and am only buying one book for composting, I’d get this one.

https://www.amazon.com/Composting-New-Generation-Latest-Techniques/dp/1591866928/

This book has a competent editor. The materials are presented methodologically. The pictures are big and clear. The text is in clear sections. The page designs are much more modern compared to other compost books. If there is any complaint, it would be that the book is too big and heavy. This book is suitable for graphic learners who wants easy access to detail explanations at the same time.

https://www.amazon.com/Compost-forma-natural-preparar-huerto/dp/8480768258/

This book feels more like a coffee table book. It’s one of those feel-good books that you read on a subject that you already know. The pages include subject lines and there are enough graphics in the book that help you to breeze through it.

https://www.amazon.com/Compost-City-Practical-Composting-Small-Space/dp/1611802202/

This books offer more specific composting ideas for people who live in the urban setting. The first half of the book aim to combat people’s hesitation about composting in a more dense living area. I find the book inspiring but this was not the book I relied on for the know-how.

https://www.amazon.com/Good-Soil-Manure-Compost-Nourishment/dp/0711238723/

This book is the ultimate coffee table book that would match your farmhouse chic coffee table. The book is full of beautiful full page photos of flowers, leaves, and farmhouse animals. This book is more than compost, but rather a lifestyle book. This book will make a good gift for your aspiring hobby farmer friend.

https://www.amazon.com/Second-Nature-Gardeners-Michael-Pollan-ebook/dp/B008UX8M9U/

This book has a chapter on “Compost and its moral imperatives.” It’s fascinating to read phillosophical take on composting. The author describes composting as a “pretty silly fetish”. This chapter is an interesting read if you want to see how the psychology of composting can be theorized.

https://www.amazon.com/Composting-Bobs-Basics-Bob-Flowerdew/dp/1616085916/

This book is one of my favorite books about composting because the pictures are real. Many of the compost books show compost piles as this perfect fluffy uniform earth-colored piles ready for a photoshoot. This book is different. The pictures of the compost piles in this book are messy, imperfect, rustic like a REAL compost pile! Thank you, Bob!

https://www.amazon.com/Rodale-Composting-Newly-Revised-Updated/dp/1635651026/

Rodale equals prestige in composting to me. I like that this book is very comprehensive and offer both width and depth on the subject. This is the book that I aspire to read word-for-word every page. But to be honest, it will end up being that book I will aspire to read but forever left on the book shelf. The book presents composting in a dry manner and makes it hard to focus.

https://www.amazon.com/Smith-Hawken-Gardener-Composting-Hands/dp/0761107320/

I like this book for its elegant writing and its nifty size. The language is beautiful and concise. The book itself is small and thin and thus making it approachable. This book ended up being the book I refer to the most. “It’s part a science, but mostly an art, composting can be a very rewarding activity.”

https://www.amazon.com/Complete-Compost-Gardening-Guide-techniques/dp/1580177026/

This book is the closest to the encyclopedia of compost. You can find almost all aspects of compost know-how in this book. You won’t come to this book for teh step by step instruction but will refer to this book when you have a question and know you can definitely find an answer.

https://www.amazon.com/Secrets-Gardening-Illustrated-Elizabeth-1998-01-02/dp/B01FGOYGHM/

This book is best used as a reference book. There is not a strong narrative or story telling aspect to it but rather matter of fact, clear, step-by-step presentation of each subject. For a reference book on the bookshelf, I’d use this book or this book.

https://www.amazon.com/Community-Scale-Composting-Systems-Comprehensive-Practical/dp/1603586547/

This book is written people who want to compost with others at a larger scale. The first time I read this book, I did it out of curiosity of how to scale a composting operation. The second time i read this book, I had just came back from a tour at Maryland Organic Composting facility where they could process up to 32,500 tons of compost a year. The first time I was fascinated. The second time, I was impressed, by the knowledge and how pragmatic the book was written. Highly recommend this book if you want to learn how to recycle organics at the community scale.
